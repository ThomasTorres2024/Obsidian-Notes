# [[Ring Isomorphism]]
A [[Ring Isomorphism|Ring Isomorphism]] is a [[Function]] that is [[bijective]] [[Ring Homomorphism]] between two rings $A$ and $B$. In symbols we denote this for function $\phi$ as:
$$\begin{align}
\frac{R}{\text{ker}(\phi)}\longrightarrow \phi(R) \\
r+\text{ker}(\phi) \mapsto \phi(r)
\end{align}$$
Is an isomorphism. Hence:
$$\frac{R}{\text{ker}(\phi)} \cong \text{Im}(\phi)$$
