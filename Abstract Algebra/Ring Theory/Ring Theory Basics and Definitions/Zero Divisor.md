# Zero Divisor
A [[Zero Divisor]], $a$ is a non-zero element of a commutative [[Ring]] $R$ such that there is another non zero element of $R$, $b$ satisfies that:
$$ab=0$$
Where $0$ is the [[Identity Element]] for addition under the ring. 

---
## Example 10.) $\mathbb{Z}_{5}[i]=\{a+bi : a,b \in \mathbb{Z}_{5},i^2=-1 \}$
The Gaussian integers mod $5$ are a ring with $25$ elements, but they are not an integral domain, since:
$$(1+2i)(1-2i)=1-4i^2=0$$
