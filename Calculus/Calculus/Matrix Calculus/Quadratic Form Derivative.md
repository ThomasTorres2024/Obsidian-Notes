# Quadratic Form Derivative 
Recall the definition of a [[Quadratic Form]] given a [[Symmetric Matrix]] $A \in \mathbb{R}^{n \times n}$, then for $\vec{ x} \in \mathbb{R}$ we have that the quadratic form of $A$ is:
$$\vec{x}^T\vec{A}x = \sum_{i=1}^n \sum_{i=1}^j ax_{i}x_{j}$$
We define the [[Quadratic Form Derivative]] by:
$$\nabla(\vec{x}^T A \vec{x})=2A \vec{x} \implies \nabla\left( \frac{1}{2} \vec{x}^T \vec{A}x \right)=\vec{A}x$$
